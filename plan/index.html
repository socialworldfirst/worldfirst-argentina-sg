01

Overview and objective

WorldFirst is the Official Regional Sponsor of the Argentine national team. The signed-jersey prize draw is the hook; the real goal is World Card acquisition and spend across Southeast Asia. This plan times paid against the World Cup so the brand is loud when attention is highest, then converts when intent is warmest.

  • Two objectives, run separately: brand awareness at the World Cup peak, and qualified World Card sign-ups in the weeks after.
  • Markets: Malaysia (lead), Singapore, Thailand, Indonesia, Philippines. Vietnam runs on its own channel.
  • Window: now to 17 Aug; prize draw 31 Aug. The final on 19 Jul is the attention peak.
  • Compliance: Messi's visual is AFA-licensed; never named in copy. No "World Cup" or "FIFA" wording on creative.
02

What the pilot taught us

A US$542 pilot (MY/SG/VN, Facebook, optimised for link clicks) gave us the engine for everything below, and two clear warnings.

  • Cheap clicks are not customers. Vietnam drove the most, cheapest clicks (US$0.03, 8% CTR) and produced zero sign-ups; the prize pulled football fans, not businesses.
  • The converters were MY and SG. All 3 completed sign-ups came from them. Blended benchmarks: CPM US$1.11, US$54 per sign-up start, US$143 per completed; MY about 6× cheaper than SG.
  • The journey leaks. "Get a World Card" drops new users at a login wall; land to sign-up was just 0.2%. The router fix is queued.

So round two changes three things: optimise for sign-ups not clicks, split awareness from conversion, and fix the login-wall journey.

03

Strategy, two jobs not one

Payments is a "when-needed" category: at any moment only about 5% are in-market. You can't convert the other 95% today, but you can be the name they remember when the need arrives.

Awareness, the 95%

  • Messi as the fame device, broad reach
  • Job is to build memory, measured on reach and branded search, not sign-ups
  • Runs hot through the knockouts, peaks at the final

Conversion, the 5%

  • Product-led, optimised for sign-ups (needs the pixel)
  • Retargets the pool awareness just warmed, plus seller interests
  • Harvests in the afterglow, through the draw
The flywheel. Cheap broad awareness warms a huge pool, that pool becomes the retargeting audience, conversion harvests it. Build the audience once at peak attention, harvest it for weeks. Post-final, hand it to the paid team for deeper product conversion (shared via Business Manager).

06

Regional split and audience saturation

Priority split: MY 50%, SG 25%, TH 10%, ID 10%, PH 5%, weighted to the lead World Card markets. This concentrates budget on the smaller audiences, so MY and SG are the saturation risk. "Days to cover" assumes broad targeting.

MarketDailyShareBroad reach 25-55Impr / dayDays to cover oncePrimary job
Malaysia—50%~15M——Lead, awareness + convert
Singapore—25%~3M——Convert, premium, capped
Thailand—10%~30M——Reach, headroom
Indonesia—10%~70M——Reach, headroom
Philippines—5%~45M——Reach, new market
This split saturates the two lead markets fastest. 75% on MY and SG concentrates spend where the audience is smallest. Even broad, MY covers its whole reachable audience in about 8 days, SG in about 9 (roughly 3 to 4× frequency over a full run). Run it narrow and they burn out in about a day. So broad is mandatory, hold a frequency cap, and pivot MY and SG to conversion from about day 8 to 10.

How to spend US$3,000/day without burning the audience:

  • Go broad: geo, age, Advantage+ audience. Narrow ICP cannot absorb the budget and chokes delivery.
  • Frequency cap about 2 to 3 per week; broad gives the room to honour it.
  • Widen placements: Feed, Stories, Reels, Instagram, Audience Network, more inventory.
  • Rotate all 5 creatives; at this frequency they fight wear-out (at low budget 2 was right, at US$3k/day, 5).
  • Saturation is the pivot signal: when reach plateaus and frequency climbs, shift that market awareness to conversion. MY and SG trip it first.
  • TH, ID, PH are the relief valve: huge headroom if MY and SG run hot.
07

Creative, two Messi sets, all 5 in rotation

10 approved assets (5 designs, standard and card). At US$3k/day the audience sees ads often, so we rotate all 5 for freshness, but the two Messi-led sets are the core (concentration beats spread for learning).

  • Awareness: Messi standard frames (Poster 2 centre, Poster 4 back). Maximum stopping power for the fame job.
  • Conversion: the card variants (product cue), plus 2 to 3 briefed product-led cuts ("no FX fees, 1% cashback").
  • SF and final hook: "the captain's last dance", likely his last World Cup match, the strongest emotional cut of the campaign.
  • Compliance: Messi's visual is licensed, the copy never names him. No "World Cup" or "FIFA" wording. WF lock-up stays prominent (guards the celebrity vampire effect).
08

Targeting and audience

Awareness, broad

  • Geo, age 25 to 55, Advantage+ audience
  • Advantage+ placements (Feed, Stories, Reels, IG, Audience Network)
  • Frequency cap about 2 to 3 per week
  • No narrow interest cage, it cannot absorb the budget

Conversion, tight

  • Seller interest steer: Shopee, Lazada, Shopify, Amazon, dropshipping
  • Retarget awareness engagers and landing visitors
  • Lookalike of the pilot sign-ups (seed, then scale)
  • MY and SG primary; pivot in from about day 8 to 10
09

Measurement and KPIs

  • Awareness: reach, frequency (2 to 3 per week), CPM, and branded-search lift in each market. Capture a baseline now. Not judged on sign-ups.
  • Conversion: cost per landing-page-view, then cost per sign-up once the pixel is live, plus land to sign-up rate (the router's impact).
  • Live tracking: a shared Google Sheet joins Meta spend and clicks to the GA sign-up funnel, refreshed routinely, so every team reads the same numbers.
10

Running it live

  • Pre-approve the per-day rate; the meter runs each day Argentina are alive, no re-approval per round, and stops the moment they exit.
  • Match-day burst: an extra push in the 24 to 48 hours after each Argentina win, when the spike is highest.
  • Saturation pivot: shift MY and SG from awareness to conversion around day 8 to 10.
  • Hand-off: post-final, share the warm audience to the paid team (Business Manager) for deep product conversion through the draw.
  • Prerequisites (fast-follow): the Meta pixel and the journey router go live before conversion ramps.
11

Risks and dependencies

  • Argentina advancing: the budget tracks the run, adaptive, stops on exit. Likely deep (holders, Messi in record form) but not guaranteed.
  • Total exposure: at US$3,000/day the range is about US$42k (out R32) to US$90k (champions). Confirm.
  • Philippines is new: verify World Card availability for PH businesses and route PH to the SEA landing.
  • Pixel not yet live: conversion optimises on landing-page-views until it is.
  • Audience saturation in MY and SG, managed via broad, cap, and pivot (section 06).
