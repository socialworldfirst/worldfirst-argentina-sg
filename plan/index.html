Win a signed Argentina jersey — riding the World Cup to World Card sign-ups

Ride the 2026 World Cup attention peak (Messi, now the all-time top scorer, likely his last) to build brand memory, then harvest World Card sign-ups in the afterglow. Built on real pilot data, with a live budget model you can dial below.

01

Overview & objective

WorldFirst is the Official Regional Sponsor of the Argentine national team. The "win a signed Argentina jersey" prize-draw is the hook; the real goal is World Card acquisition and spend across Southeast Asia. This plan times paid against the World Cup so the brand is loud when attention is highest, then converts when intent is warmest.

  • Two objectives, run separately: brand awareness at the World Cup peak, and qualified World Card sign-ups in the weeks after.
  • Markets: Malaysia (lead) · Singapore · Thailand · Indonesia · Philippines. Vietnam runs on its own channel.
  • Window: now → 17 Aug; prize draw 31 Aug. The World Cup final (19 Jul) is the attention peak.
  • Compliance: Messi's visual is AFA-licensed; never named in copy. No "World Cup / FIFA" wording on creative.
02

What the pilot taught us

A US$542 pilot (MY/SG/VN, Facebook, optimised for link clicks) gave us the engine for everything below — and two clear warnings.

  • Cheap clicks ≠ customers. Vietnam drove the most, cheapest clicks (US$0.03, 8% CTR) and produced zero sign-ups — the prize pulled football fans, not businesses.
  • The converters were MY + SG. All 3 completed sign-ups came from them. Benchmarks (blended): CPM US$1.11, US$54 per sign-up start, US$143 per completed; MY ~6× cheaper than SG.
  • The journey leaks. "Get a World Card" dumps new users at a login wall — land→sign-up was just 0.2%. The router fix is queued.

So round two changes three things: optimise for sign-ups, not clicks; split awareness from conversion; and fix the login-wall journey.

03

Strategy — two jobs, not one

Payments is a "when-needed" category: at any moment only ~5% are in-market. You can't convert the other 95% today, but you can be the name they remember when the need arrives. So we run two jobs and let the audience flow from one to the other.

Awareness — the 95%

  • Messi as the fame device, broad reach
  • Job = build memory, measured on reach + branded search, not sign-ups
  • Runs hot through the knockouts, peaks at the final

Conversion — the 5%

  • Product-led, optimised for sign-ups (needs the pixel)
  • Retargets the pool awareness just warmed + seller interests
  • Harvests in the afterglow, through the draw
The flywheel: cheap broad awareness warms a huge pool → that pool becomes the retargeting audience → conversion harvests it. Build the audience once at peak attention, harvest it for weeks. Post-final, hand that warm audience to the paid/performance team for deeper product conversion (shared via Business Manager).

06

Regional split & audience saturation

Split at this daily rate + saturation

Priority split — MY 50% · SG 25% · TH 10% · ID 10% · PH 5%, weighted to the lead World Card markets. This concentrates budget on the smaller audiences, so MY + SG are the saturation risk. "Days to cover" assumes broad targeting.

The saturation problem: 75% on MY + SG concentrates spend where the audience is smallest — even broad, MY covers its whole reachable audience in ~8 days, SG in ~9 (≈3–4× frequency over a full run). Run it narrow and they burn out in ~1 day. So broad is mandatory, hold a frequency cap, and pivot MY + SG to conversion/retargeting from ~day 8–10.

How to spend US$3,000/day without burning the audience:

  • Go broad — geo + age + Advantage+ audience. Narrow ICP can't absorb the budget and chokes delivery.
  • Frequency cap ~2–3/week; broad gives the room to honour it.
  • Widen placements — FB Feed + Stories + Reels + IG + Audience Network = more inventory.
  • Rotate all 5 creatives — at this frequency they fight wear-out (at low budget 2 was right; at US$3k/day, 5).
  • Saturation = pivot signal — when reach plateaus and frequency climbs, shift that market awareness → conversion. MY/SG trip it first (~day 8–10).
  • TH/ID/PH are the relief valve — huge headroom if MY/SG run hot.
07

Creative — two Messi sets, all 5 in rotation

10 approved assets (5 designs × standard + card). At US$3k/day the audience sees ads often, so we rotate all 5 for freshness — but the two Messi-led sets are the core (concentration beats spread for learning).

  • Awareness = Messi standard frames (Poster 2 centre, Poster 4 back). Maximum stopping power for the fame job.
  • Conversion = the card variants (product cue) + 2–3 briefed product-led cuts ("no FX fees · 1% cashback").
  • SF/Final hook = "the captain's last dance" — likely his last World Cup match, the strongest emotional cut of the campaign.
  • Compliance = Messi's visual is licensed; the copy never names him. No "World Cup / FIFA" wording. WF lock-up stays prominent (guards the celebrity "vampire" effect).
08

Targeting & audience

Awareness — broad

  • Geo + age 25–55 + Advantage+ audience
  • Advantage+ placements (Feed, Stories, Reels, IG, Audience Network)
  • Frequency cap ~2–3/week
  • No narrow interest cage — it can't absorb the budget

Conversion — tight

  • Seller interest steer: Shopee, Lazada, Shopify, Amazon, dropshipping
  • Retarget awareness engagers + landing visitors
  • Lookalike of the pilot sign-ups (seed, then scale)
  • MY + SG primary; pivot in from ~day 8–10
09

Measurement & KPIs

  • Awareness: reach, frequency (≤2–3/wk), CPM, and branded-search lift in each market — capture a baseline now. Not judged on sign-ups.
  • Conversion: cost per landing-page-view → cost per sign-up once the pixel is live, plus land→sign-up rate (the router's impact).
  • Live tracking: a shared Google Sheet joins Meta spend/clicks to the GA sign-up funnel, refreshed routinely, so any team (and the other office) reads the same numbers.
10

Running it live

  • Pre-approve the per-day rate — the meter runs each day Argentina are alive, no re-approval per round; it stops the moment they exit.
  • Match-day burst: an extra push in the 24–48h after each Argentina win, when the spike is highest.
  • Saturation pivot: shift MY + SG from awareness → conversion/retargeting around day 8–10.
  • Hand-off: post-final, share the warm audience to the paid/performance team (Business Manager) for deep product conversion through the draw.
  • Prerequisites (fast-follow): the Meta pixel + the journey router go live before conversion ramps.
11

Risks & dependencies

  • Argentina advancing — the budget tracks the run; adaptive, stops on exit. Likely deep (holders, Messi in record form) but not guaranteed.
  • Total exposure — at US$3,000/day the range is ~US$42k (out R32) to ~US$90k (champions). Confirm.
  • Philippines is new — verify World Card availability/eligibility for PH businesses and route PH to the SEA landing.
  • Pixel not yet live — conversion optimises on landing-page-views until it is.
  • Audience saturation in MY/SG (managed via broad + cap + pivot, section 06).
